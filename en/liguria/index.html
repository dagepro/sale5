The Liguria is a small region: an arc of 270 kilometers, from the mouth of the Magra at the French border, almost completely mountainous (65% of the territory) and hilly (35%). Nevertheless, the landscape is very varied, so much natural aspects, than in those due to the work of human hands. The coastal area is the most populated, where most of the Hotels in Liguria, While inland crops give way to the oak and chestnut woods, in turn replaced by forests and pastures of the Maritime Alps and the Ligurian Apennines.

Liguria in pills: the geography

Liguria is a continuous succession of bays and inlets: The stretch of the Tyrrhenian Sea embraced by the region is called the The Gulf of Genoa, While the coast West of Genoa, It is called Riviera di Ponente and the East is called Riviera di Levante.

The mountain system – Only as regards the geological formation can make a distinction between the Alps and the Apennines. The welding spot between the Maritime Alps, following the border with France, and the Ligurian Apennines is conventionally identified with the Passo di Cadibona, West of Savona. The Maritime Alps have some peaks above 2.000 meters.
The Ligurian Apennines does not reach the 2.000 meters. The highest peaks are mount Penna (1.735 meters) and Monte Antola (1.597 meters). The watershed is quite far from the coast West of Genoa, If you open the larger valleys. It is very close to the coast of the Levant. The Ligurian mountains are easily with penetrable boundaries because the steps are all of modest elevation. In addition to the Colle di Cadibona, for which passes the road Savona-Ceva linking Liguria the Piedmont, the main steps are: the Passo dei Giovi (motorway Genoa-Serravalle Scrivia-Milano); the pitch of the Ligurian Apennines (Highway Genoa-Piacenza); the Bracco pass, critical to road connections between Liguria and Tuscany.

The waterways – Liguria has real rivers. Short rivers that come down from the mountains to the sea have torrential, Spring and summer filled with dried. From West to East the succession is as follows: the Roia, He was born in France and flows at Ventimiglia; the Empire that empties into the Tyrrhenian Sea between Porto Maurizio and Oneglia (Imperia); the Polcevera, coming down from Passo dei Giovi and flows to San Pier d'arena (Sampierdarena), to the West of the city centre of Genoa; the Bisagno; the Entella; the Vara, that flows into the Magra, whose course, which began in Tuscany, ends Southeast of La Spezia. Born on the Ligurian mountains, but down to the Po, the Tanaro, the Bormida, the Scrivia, The Trebbia and the Staffora.

The climate – The arc of mountains formed from the Maritime Alps and the Ligurian Apennines protects it from the Northern winds the coastline, that is exposed to the influence cooling influence of the sea. The weather is so warm in winter, not terribly hot in summer, Although somewhat rainy weather because moisture condense mountains caused by evaporation marina. These climatic conditions favor the development of typical Mediterranean vegetation, as is found in much lower latitudes.
