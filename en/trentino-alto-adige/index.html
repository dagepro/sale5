The Trentino Alto Adige region is rich both in terms of cultural tourism. No shortage of numerous Hotels in Trentino Alto Adige, but also bed and breakfast, Holiday cottages and the famous Masi, Classic homes of the area, for accommodation.

On the map the appearance is roughly that of a large Wye formed by the conjunction, at the height of Bolzano, of lsarco Valley, coming down from Brenner, through the course of’ Adige that from Reschen pass runs through the Venosta Valley and continues towards Trento e Verona. In the two main grooves converge many valleys: Some have great renown tourism, as the Val Gardena or the Val Pusteria; the others are places without too much fuss offer thrills and unforgettable landscapes.

The relief is constituted by the solid bastion of Eastern Alps, who here start staying on average 2500 meters, but there are frequent elevations exceeding 3000. From the Alps we then move on to the foothills of the Alps and the Dolomites with the most spectacular at this famous ski area.

Abundant everywhere fauna: by the bear to the marmot, Since roe deer, The Ibex suede, by grouse at the Golden Eagle. Some hotels in Trentino Alto Adige organize guided tours to discover these magnificent animals and their natural habitat.

Shared with the Lombardy is the Stelvio National Park, certificate around the Group deIl ' Ortles-Cevedale, which were added over time various regional parks to set up a wildlife sanctuary alpina which develops continuous, by connecting the main formations of the relief.

Trentino has only two provinces: Bolzano and Trento. The Salurner Klause represents the physical border between Trentino region, mainly Venetian culture, and Bolzano, where they speak German and are kept alive the social structures and traditions of Habsburg source, Hence the distinction, that we find in the administrative designation, of Trentino and South Tyrol.

The region has an economy traditionally based on’Agriculture and on a Tourism that at least the most important living stations, as Bressanone and Meran, boasts a centuries-old tradition. More recent is the development of rural hospitality, Moreover, as a natural expression of the potential of an experienced territory which alternates to the landscapes of the Valley, carpeted with vineyards and orchards, the grass in height, where everything revolves around breeding cattle and dairy production top notch.

In South Tyrol, As a matter of fact, l’rural reception It was already an established tradition before the boom of the agriturismo. The business has grown in recent years, but not for this changed its connotation and even though accommodation is offered increasingly in recent buildings, the whole country life continues to unfold around the Masi, the typical construction of the Valley dwellers, with stone base, used as a cowshed, and wooden superstructure divided between dwelling and barn. Around is the vegetable garden, always very flowery, While a little farther lie the fields, giving potatoes and cereals adapt to climate, as the rye, used in the production of the typical black bread. Trentino Alto Adige in pills: the geography

The northern part of the region, in the province of Bolzano, is called South Tyrol; the southern part, the Trentino, takes its name from the capital Trento.

The Alps: The region is closed to the North by the main bastion of the Alpine chain, and precisely from the initial part of the Eastern Alps. The average altitude is 2.500 meters but several peaks exceed 3.000. At Brenner the altitude drops to 1.375 meters; the Saddle of Dobbiaco, connecting the Valley with the Drava, the altitude drops to 1.170 meters. These two passes, make communications easier than those with Austria Lombardy.
The main peaks of the Northern bastion are white or White Ball blade (3.736 meters) in Ötztal Alps, the Hochfeiler (3.510 meters) in the Zillertal. In the West there are the groups of' Ortles-Cevedale and Adamello, rich both of glaciers. Among the Adamello and Trent is the Group of Brenta.
To orient the Trentino Alto Adige is occupied by Dolomites, long chain of limestone mountains and pointy little stubby shapes. The main peak is the Marmolada (3.354 meters); other known groups are the Rosengarten, the Saddle and the Sassolungo.

Prealpi: Occupy a small part of the region to the East and South of Trento with the mountains of Valsugana and around Lake Garda with mount Altissimo.

The rivers and valleys: Trentino Alto Adige is rich in water. The river Adige (410 km to the mouth) It is the second longest river in Italy and across the region. The only major rivers are tributaries of the Adige are not the Sarca, that flows into Lake Garda, and the Brenta, that comes from the Lakes of Levico and Caldonazzo, drops in the Veneto region and flows into the Adriatic Sea. The region, It belongs to the northern end of Lake Garda, is rich in mountain lakes: the best known are those of Caress, Ledro and Tovel. There are also several reservoirs for hydroelectric energy production.

The climate: Typical of mountain areas: very cold winters and cool summers, with the usual phenomenon of thermal inversion, to which temperatures in the Valley, less exposed to the Sun, are lower than those of the higher areas.
